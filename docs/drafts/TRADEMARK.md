## Why an unregistered mark still protects the name

In most jurisdictions, trademark rights arise from **using a distinctive name and logo in
commerce** — registration strengthens and broadens those rights but is not required for them
to exist. Because MailPoppy uses the name and logo publicly as its brand, the owner can object
to uses that are likely to confuse people about the source of the software. A registration may
be filed in the future; until then, these common-law rights apply.
